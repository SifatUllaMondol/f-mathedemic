Probability

Learn Probability easily with examples and visual explanations. Understand the likelihood of events and how to calculate them.

What is Probability?

Probability is the measure of how likely an event is to happen. It is a number between 0 and 1 (or 0% to 100%):

  • 0 → Impossible event
  • 1 → Certain event

Formula:

P(Event) = Number of favorable outcomes / Total number of possible outcomes

Example 1: Tossing a Coin

A fair coin has 2 sides: Head and Tail.

Event: Getting a Head

Favorable outcomes: 1 (Head) / Total outcomes: 2 (Head or Tail)

P(Head) = 1/2 = 0.5 = 50%

Example 2: Rolling a Dice

A standard die has 6 faces numbered 1 to 6.

Event: Rolling a 4

P(4) = 1/6 ≈ 0.1667 = 16.67%

Event: Rolling an even number (2, 4, 6)

P(Even) = 3/6 = 0.5 = 50%

Example 3: Drawing Cards

A standard deck has 52 cards.

Event: Drawing an Ace

P(Ace) = 4/52 = 1/13 ≈ 7.69%

Event: Drawing a Heart

P(Heart) = 13/52 = 1/4 = 25%

Example 4: Combined Events

Independent events: Events that do not affect each other.

Tossing a coin twice, probability of getting two heads:

P(Head and Head) = 0.5 × 0.5 = 0.25 = 25%

Mutually exclusive events: Events that cannot happen together.

Rolling a dice, probability of 2 or 5:

P(2 or 5) = 1/6 + 1/6 = 1/3 ≈ 33.33%
